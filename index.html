Privacy Policy

The Magic Diary for iPad · Last updated 11 July 2026

The Magic Diary does not collect, transmit, or store any personal information about you. Everything you write stays on your iPad.

What the app stores

Your handwriting, the text recognized from it, and the diary's replies are saved only in the app's local storage on your device. They are never uploaded to us or to anyone else. We have no servers, no accounts, and no way to see what you write.

How replies are generated

Handwriting recognition and reply generation happen entirely on your iPad using Apple's on-device frameworks (Vision and Apple Intelligence). No API key is required and no internet connection is needed. Your writing is not sent to any external AI service.

The optional "Search the internet" setting

If you turn on Search the internet in Settings, the app may send a short search query derived from your writing to DuckDuckGo and Wikipedia in order to look up factual information. Only that search query leaves your device — never your full diary entry, your history, or any identifying information. This setting can be turned off at any time, and no search occurs while it is off. Those services handle the query under their own privacy policies.

What we do not do

  • No analytics or usage tracking
  • No advertising and no ad identifiers
  • No third-party SDKs that collect data
  • No accounts, sign-in, or email collection
  • No selling or sharing of data — we never receive any

Deleting your data

You can delete any saved page from within the app, turn off Remember conversations so nothing is saved at all, or delete the app to remove everything from your device. Because your entries exist only on your iPad, deleting them removes them permanently.

Children

The app does not knowingly collect information from anyone, including children under 13, because it does not collect information at all.
